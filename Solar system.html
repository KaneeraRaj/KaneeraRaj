    • Welcome To Kaneera's tour to Solar System

    The solar system is a collection of celestial bodies gravitationally bound to the Sun, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. It formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing cloud of gas and dust, forming the Sun and a protoplanetary disc. The Sun, a yellow dwarf star, contains 99.8% of the solar system's mass, and everything else orbits it. Lets go through all the planets of the Solar System

    Mercury

    Mercury is the smallest and fastest planet in our solar system, and the closest to the Sun. It's a rocky planet with a heavily cratered surface, similar to the Moon, and has a thin exosphere. Mercury's surface temperatures vary greatly, from extreme heat on the sunlit side to extreme cold on the shadowed side

    Venus

    Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is known for being incredibly hot, with a surface temperature hot enough to melt lead. It's often called Earth's "twin" or "sister" planet due to its similar size and structure. Venus is also the brightest planet in the solar system

    Earth

    Earth is the third planet from the Sun, the only known celestial body to harbor life. It's a rocky, terrestrial planet with one moon, and it's the fifth largest in our solar system. Earth is a unique "ocean planet" with water covering about 70% of its surface

    Mars

    Mars, often called the "Red Planet," is the fourth planet from the Sun in our solar system. It's a rocky, desert-like planet with a thin atmosphere, mostly composed of carbon dioxide. Mars has a distinct red color due to iron oxide (rust) in its soil.

    Jupiter

    Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and the fifth planet from the Sun. It's a gas giant, meaning it's primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and doesn't have a solid surface. Jupiter is known for its vibrant cloud stripes, its Great Red Spot (a giant storm), and its numerous moons.

    Saturn

    Saturn is the sixth planet from the sun and the second largest in our solar system, known for its spectacular ring system. It's a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and has numerous moons, including Titan, one of the most intriguing in our solar system

    Uranus

    Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, an ice giant primarily composed of water, ammonia, and methane in solid form. It's known for its unique axial tilt, making it appear to rotate on its side. Uranus has a blue-green hue due to methane in its atmosphere and is the third largest planet in our solar system

    Neptune

    Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun, classified as an ice giant. It's the fourth-largest planet by diameter and third-most-massive in the Solar System. Neptune is also known as the "blue giant" due to its vivid color, which is caused by methane in its atmosphere absorbing red light
