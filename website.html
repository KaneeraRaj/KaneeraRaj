Greek Pizza

Greek pizza, in the United States, refers to a style of pizza made in a shallow metal pan, with a crust that is airy, spongy, and light, similar to focaccia. It's often oily due to the olive oil used to coat the pan during baking. In Greece, "ladenia" (also known as Greek pizza) is a flatbread dish with Greek salad ingredients, often cooked in a shallow pan with olive oil.

Silicone Pizza

A "silicone pizza" refers to a non-edible silicone disc used for practicing pizza tossing and other acrobatic pizza skills. It's designed to resemble a pizza dough and is made of silicone, allowing for easy throwing, spinning, and other maneuvers without the risk of wasting food. These silicone pizzas are used by both beginners and professionals to hone their pizza-making skills and perform acrobatic tricks.
